Synopsis: Novice bounty hunter Chateau Dankworth finds herself unexpectedly overpowered in combat by a mysterious man who takes a keen interest in her. Despite her reluctance to engage with him personally, Chateau's company assigns her to eliminate Song Ryang-ha, a skilled assassin and former member of a powerful Asian organization—coincidentally, the very man pursuing her.

In a surprising turn of events, Chateau strikes a deal with Ryang-ha: she agrees to go out with him in exchange for information on current targets. However, as their pasts catch up with them, their arrangement puts both their lives in danger, prompting them to reconsider their partnership.

It's evident from the last episode that the story of Dog Signal is far from over, leaving viewers eager for more. While the anime adaptation is not without flaws, it captures the essence of the psychological manga, albeit with some missed opportunities for further exploration.

The anime delves into the characters' backgrounds and the events surrounding them, interwoven with occasional action sequences. However, some viewers may find the pacing slow compared to more action-packed anime series.

One of the anime's strengths lies in its mysterious characters, whose enigmatic nature draws viewers in. As their mysteries unravel, their complex relationships and interactions become increasingly intriguing. While some may find their chemistry unconventional, it aligns with the psychological romance genre's tendency towards atypical relationships.

The sound design, including the opening and ending themes, effectively complements the anime's atmosphere. Despite its shortcomings, many viewers find enjoyment in the series, although some express disappointment with its premature conclusion.
