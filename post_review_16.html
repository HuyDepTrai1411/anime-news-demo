Momochi-san Chi no Ayakashi Ouji

The Demon Prince of Momochi House

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #16

Type: TV Serie

Status: Currently Airing

Aired: Jan 6, 2024 to Mar 23, 2024

Episodes: 12

Premiered: Winter 2024

Synopsis: On her 16th birthday, Himari Momochi inherits the "Momochi House," the only link to her real family. Unfazed by rumors of hauntings, Himari encounters Aoi Nanamori and his companions, Ise and Yukari, squatting in her new home. Despite warnings to leave, Himari resolves to evict them instead.

However, she soon discovers the house is a haven for youkai and straddles the border between the living and spirit realms. Aoi, chosen as its guardian, reveals that Himari was meant for the role, but Aoi is trapped within the mansion, his existence erased from the human world. Determined to help Aoi break the curse, Himari stays to live alongside him.

I don't usually write reviews, but for Demon Prince of Momochi House, I had to share my thoughts because this anime deserves more positive attention and discussion.

Having read the manga before the anime's release, I approached the adaptation with certain expectations. However, it's important to note that the anime deviates from the manga and compresses its content into 12 episodes. While it covers most major plot points, it inevitably sacrifices some depth and pacing.

Teaser visual

Shoujo anime often face this challenge due to limited episode counts and low chances of second seasons. Momochi House would have benefited from a longer run to fully capture its rich storyline and character development.

Character-driven rather than plot-driven, the anime delves into the characters' histories, experiences, and traumas. If you enjoy stories focused on character growth and interpersonal dynamics, this is worth watching.

Despite criticisms of the animation, I found it faithful to the manga's art style, vibrant, and fluid in action scenes. The character designs and supernatural Japanese mythology aesthetic add to the atmosphere.

Trailer

The characters remain true to their manga counterparts, with Himari standing out as a strong heroine. Contrary to comparisons, Aoi/Nue possesses his own distinct personality. While most characters shine, the antagonist Kasha falls short.

The music, particularly the OP, adds to the overall experience. Fans of similar anime like Kamisama Kiss and Natsume Yujinchou will likely enjoy Demon Prince of Momochi House.
