Suki tte Ii na yo.

Say "I Love You."

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #9

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Oct 7, 2012 to Dec 30, 2012

Episodes: 13

Premiered: Fall 2012

Synopsis: Mei Tachibana has always believed that friends will only let her down, a belief reinforced by a childhood incident where she was wrongfully blamed for a class pet's death. As a result, she isolates herself from others to avoid getting hurt again. However, Mei's life takes a turn when she meets popular student Yamato Kurosawa after a misunderstanding in high school. Despite Mei's reluctance, Yamato is determined to befriend her. When Yamato saves Mei from a stalker by kissing her, Mei begins to develop feelings for him.

As Mei and Yamato start dating, Mei struggles to adapt to her new lifestyle and express her true feelings. Despite her challenges, Mei gains not only a boyfriend but also friends, challenging her belief that she's better off alone.

While I wanted to love "Sukitte Ii na yo," it fell short of my expectations as a two-dimensional romance with untapped potential. Despite offering some unique elements, the series ultimately disappoints.

The premise follows the unlikely romance between Mei Tachibana, a shy loner, and Yamato Kurosawa, the popular guy in school. Their relationship begins after a misunderstanding but lacks depth throughout the series.

Teaser visual

Unlike typical romance anime, Mei and Yamato are in a relationship from the start. However, the development of their love lacks depth and feels underwhelming. The series focuses more on why they love each other rather than their romantic journey.

While I enjoyed watching "Sukitte Ii na yo," it pales in comparison to other romance anime. The main characters, Mei and Yamato, are average and lack depth. Mei's character development is clichéd, while Yamato's personality feels inconsistent.

Trailer

The supporting cast is stereotypical, and the plot repeats familiar themes without adding depth. However, the series excels in artwork and soundtrack, with fluid animation and memorable music.

Despite its flaws, "Sukitte Ii na yo" is still worth watching for those seeking a sweet romance with a predictable ending.
