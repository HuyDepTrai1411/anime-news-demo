Darwin's Game

ダーウィンズゲーム

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #20

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Jan 4, 2020 to Mar 21, 2020

Episodes: 11

Premiered: Winter 2020

Synopsis: Kaname Sudou, a high school student, is introduced to Darwin's Game by a classmate. Upon activating the mobile app, he is attacked by a snake avatar and loses consciousness. Awakening unharmed, he brushes it off as a hallucination until he tries the game again. This time, he faces a real-life opponent armed with a knife, revealing that Darwin's Game is a deadly battle for survival. Kaname must now navigate this dangerous game and fight to stay alive.

Darwin's Game may seem like a mishmash of familiar elements from other series, blending the death game concept of Fate Stay Night with the phone mechanic of Future Diary and the battle royale setup of Fortnite. Yet, despite its seemingly derivative nature, the series manages to deliver an enjoyable experience. While it lacks a thought-provoking narrative or deep character development, Darwin's Game excels in providing entertainment through its quirky characters and intense battle royale environment.

One notable aspect of Darwin's Game is its setting, which mirrors our own world. However, this realism raises questions about how such a large-scale survival game could exist within society and how players acquire magical abilities through a mobile app. While these questions are left unanswered in the anime adaptation, it's important to remember that the series is just beginning its exploration of Darwin's Game. It's not meant to provide all the answers but rather to establish character relationships and present intriguing scenarios.

Teaser visual

The plot follows protagonist Kaname Sudou, who is thrust into the deadly Darwin's Game after downloading the app. While the storyline is straightforward, it effectively portrays Kaname's journey as he forms alliances and grows stronger. What sets Darwin's Game apart is Kaname's approach to making allies out of former enemies, adding depth to the character dynamics.

Despite its short runtime, Darwin's Game succeeds in developing the bonds between Kaname and his allies in the Sunset Ravens clan. The group's banter and camaraderie make them endearing to the audience, despite the limited time for relationship development.

Surprisingly, the characters in Darwin's Game are relatively normal compared to other survival game series. While some may expect exaggerated character traits, the cast remains grounded, with even the main female lead, Shuka, avoiding typical yandere stereotypes. Instead, she is portrayed as a determined fighter with understandable motivations.

Trailer

While the animation and visuals may not be groundbreaking, Darwin's Game delivers solid entertainment value. The voice acting, particularly for antagonist Wang, stands out, adding depth to the characters. However, the animation quality could have been more polished, especially during battle sequences.

In conclusion, Darwin's Game may not offer anything groundbreaking, but it succeeds in providing an entertaining experience. Its blend of familiar elements and engaging characters make it a worthwhile watch for fans of the survival game genre.
