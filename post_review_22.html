Tokyo Ghoul

Toukyou Kushu

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #22

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Jul 4, 2014 to Sep 19, 2014

Episodes: 12

Premiered: Summer 2014

Synopsis: Ken Kaneki, a reserved college student in Tokyo, encounters the sinister world of ghouls after a date with a woman named Rize Kamishiro goes horribly wrong. He wakes up in a hospital to find out that he's become a human-ghoul hybrid after receiving Rize's organs in a life-saving surgery. Now, navigating his newfound identity, Kaneki must grapple with his humanity amidst a violent conflict between ghouls and government agents.

Tokyo Ghoul is a dark fantasy and seinen anime adaptation of the manga by Ishida Sui, produced by Studio Pierrot with 12 episodes. The story revolves around Kaneki Ken, an ordinary college student whose life changes after encountering Rize Kamishiro, a ghoul disguised as a human. Following a tragic incident, Kaneki undergoes a life-saving surgery that transforms him into a half-ghoul, half-human hybrid. As he navigates the ghoul world, Kaneki grapples with his humanity amidst societal conflict.

Teaser visual

The anime has a promising start but suffers from rushed pacing, skipped content, and inconsistent character development. Kaneki's portrayal as overly emotional diminishes his character depth. Additionally, changes to fight scenes and censorship during the airing detract from the viewing experience. Despite these flaws, the animation quality shines in fight sequences, and the soundtrack, particularly the opening song "Unravel," adds to the atmosphere. The story explores themes of morality and perspective, offering potential for intriguing character dynamics, though limited episodes hinder plot and character development.

Trailer

In conclusion, Tokyo Ghoul offers an intriguing premise and explores complex themes but falls short due to rushed pacing and inconsistent adaptation. New viewers may find it enjoyable, but manga readers may feel disappointed by missed opportunities and unfulfilled potential.
