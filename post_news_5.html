Duel Masters LOST Manga Gets TV Anime in Fall

Trailer reveals returning cast from Duel Masters Win anime

Trương Xuân Huy

Shigenobu Matsumoto and Yō Kanebayashi's manga, Duel Masters LOST Tsuioku no Suishō (Duel Masters LOST: Crystal of Remembrance), is set to receive a television anime adaptation by J.C. Staff this fall. The announcement was made through the opening of an official website on Friday, unveiling a trailer and visual along with the returning cast from the Duel Masters Win anime.

Shotaro Uzawa and Wataru Hatano will reprise their roles as Win Kirifuda and Abyssbell = Jashin Emperor (Jashin-kun), respectively.

In addition, a trailer for the Duel Masters special movie Ōdō-hen (Royal Road Arc), produced by J.C. Staff, was released. The full version is scheduled to debut during the live broadcast of the Duel Masters National Tournament 2023 on March 31, followed by its television airing in April. The theme song "Dōka" is provided by YurryCanon's project Tsukuyomi.

The manga Duel Masters LOST Tsuioku no Suishō premiered on the Weekly CoroCoro Comic website on February 1.

Teaser visual

"LOST" is an "alternate time story" that runs parallel to the current storyline of Duel Masters Win, depicting a never-before-seen world. It follows 16-year-old Win Kirifuda, who has lost everything and is drawn into a battle to reclaim his desired world.

The Duel Masters LOST project also includes the "Abyss Revolution Gaiden: Death Bell Crystal" card pack, which was released in Japan on February 17.

Trailer

The Duel Masters Win anime premiered in September 2022, accompanied by a new manga by Matsumoto in Monthly Coro Coro Comics in August. The ongoing sequel to the anime, titled Duel Masters Win: Duel Wars, debuted in Japan in April 2023.

Furthermore, the Duel Masters King anime series began in April 2020, followed by Duel Masters King! in April 2021, and the latest installment, Duel Masters King Max, in April 2022. These series continue the storyline initiated by the original Duel Masters anime that aired in April 2017.

Source: Duel Masters LOST Tsuioku no Suishō anime's website
