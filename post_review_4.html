Kaguya-sama wa Kokurasetai: Ultra Romantic

Kaguya-sama: Love is War - Ultra Romantic

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #4

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Apr 9, 2022 to Jun 25, 2022

Episodes: 13

Premiered: Spring 2022

Synopsis: The elite members of Shuchiin Academy's student council engage in their usual competitive antics, with president Miyuki Shirogane and vice-president Kaguya Shinomiya locked in a battle of wits to make the other confess their love. Kaguya, struggling against her strict upbringing, begins to thaw as she grows closer to Shirogane and their friends.

Meanwhile, treasurer Yuu Ishigami harbors a hopeless crush on upperclassman Tsubame Koyasu, finding newfound confidence with her encouragement. New council member Miko Iino forms a bond with Ishigami while challenging her own rigid moral code.

As romance blossoms at Shuchiin Academy, the student council members embroil their outsider friends in increasingly comedic conflicts.

Love can be complicated, frightening, and transient, but for Kaguya and Miyuki, it's akin to warfare. Their ongoing battle to make the other confess their feelings has been a source of amusement, but as time becomes a factor, they question the worth of their "war." In the face of life's uncertainties, seizing every chance to be with loved ones becomes imperative.

Watching the third season of Kaguya-sama felt like witnessing the climax of a narrative, where pivotal events would propel the characters into new chapters of their lives.

Credit is due to mangaka Aka Akasaka for the brilliant storytelling and comedic elements in the original work, but equal praise must be given to A-1 Pictures and the entire staff for bringing the manga to life with exceptional quality. The anime surpasses expectations with its meticulous adaptations and ingenious references, demonstrating a deep understanding of modern society.

The season continues the misadventures of the student council, with Kaguya and Miyuki's relationship at the forefront. While maintaining its signature comedic style, the season delves deeper into character development, particularly in the lead-up to the cultural festival.

Teaser visual

The animation is vibrant and enhances the comedy, with standout sequences like the rap segment in Episode 5. The incorporation of diverse visual references adds depth to the viewing experience.

The opening and ending themes, notably "Giri Giri," complement the series well, while the soundtrack enriches pivotal moments.

The ensemble cast shines with compelling dynamics and excellent voice acting. Noteworthy developments include Ishigami's romance subplot and the introduction of new characters like Maki Shijo and Miko Iino.

Trailer

The series consistently captivates with its humor and engaging narrative, leaving viewers eagerly awaiting each new episode.

The seamless integration of animation, music, and storytelling reinforces the thematic depth and emotional resonance of the series.

Kaguya-sama embodies a labor of love, delivering a delightful blend of romance and comedy that resonates with audiences. Its endearing characters, witty humor, and relatable themes make it a standout in the genre.
