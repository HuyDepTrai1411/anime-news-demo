A Condition Called Love Anime Reveals 2 More Cast Members, April 4 Debut

Sayumi Suzushiro, Kazuyuki Okitsu join cast

Trương Xuân Huy

The official website for the television anime adaptation of Megumi Morino's A Condition Called Love (Hananoi-kun to Koi no Yamai) manga unveiled two additional cast members for the series on Friday.

The newly announced cast includes:

  • Sayumi Suzushiro as Satomi Satomura
  • Kazuyuki Okitsu as Yukihiro Kuroe

The anime is set to premiere on April 4 on TBS' 28 affiliate channels at 11:56 p.m. JST. It will be available for streaming in Japan on ABEMA and Netflix, while Crunchyroll will stream the series simultaneously.

The main cast of the anime includes:

  • Kana Hanazawa as Hotaru Hinase
  • Chiaki Kobayashi as Saki Hananoi
  • Yurika Kubo as Hibiki "Kyō-chan" Asami
  • Maaya Sakamoto as Tsukiha "Shibamū" Shibamura
  • Ryohei Kimura as Sōhei Yao
  • Ryota Ohsaka as Keigo Kurata

Teaser visual

The anime is directed by Tomoe Makino at East Fish Studio. Hitomi Amamiya supervises and writes the series scripts, while Akiko Satō designs the characters. yamazo is responsible for composing the music. The opening theme song "Kimi no Sei" (It's Your Fault) will be performed by Sexy Zone, while the ending theme song will be "Every Second" (Japanese version) by Danish-Japanese artist Mina Okabe.

Kodansha USA Publishing began digitally publishing the manga in English in 2020 and released the first physical volume in January 2023, with the seventh volume scheduled for release on March 26. The manga follows Hotaru, a high school first-year, whose life takes an unexpected turn when her classmate Hananoi-kun asks her out after a chance encounter.

Trailer

Morino's manga debuted in Kodansha's Monthly Dessert magazine in December 2017 and has sold over 3.8 million copies. It received the Best Shōjo Manga award at Kodansha's 45th Manga Awards in 2021.

Source: A Condition Called Love anime's website
