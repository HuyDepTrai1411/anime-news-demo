Steins;Gate

STEINS;GATE

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #3

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Apr 6, 2011 to Sep 14, 2011

Episodes: 24

Premiered: Spring 2011

Synopsis: Eccentric scientist Rintarou Okabe and his friends experiment with a microwave that can send text messages into the past. However, their discovery attracts the attention of a secretive organization, putting them in danger. Now, Okabe must navigate the consequences of his invention to protect his friends and preserve the timeline.

The subject of time travel in anime is well-trodden ground, but Steins;Gate manages to set itself apart with its exceptional execution. Led by the eccentric scientist Rintarou Okabe, the story delves into the consequences of meddling with time, offering a refreshing take on the genre.

The story starts slow but gradually picks up pace, culminating in a fast-paced second half that ties up loose ends and leaves viewers satisfied. Steins;Gate meticulously adheres to its own rules of time travel, avoiding noticeable inconsistencies and delivering a compelling narrative.

Teaser visual

While the art is neither outstanding nor subpar, the voice acting stands out, particularly Okabe's portrayal by Miyano Mamoru. The soundtrack, though sparse at times, effectively enhances the mood of key scenes.

Trailer

The characters, especially Okabe, are captivating and well-developed, contributing to the overall enjoyment of the series. Steins;Gate succeeds in evoking a range of emotions, from laughter to contemplation, making it a must-watch for any anime fan.
