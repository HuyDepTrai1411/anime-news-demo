Monster

モンスター

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #14

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Apr 7, 2004 to Sep 28, 2005

Episodes: 74

Premiered: Spring 2004

Synopsis: Dr. Kenzou Tenma, a respected neurosurgeon, faces a life-changing dilemma when he chooses to save a famous performer over a poor immigrant worker at the hospital director's request. This decision triggers a crisis of conscience for Dr. Tenma. When a similar situation arises later, he defies orders to save a young boy named Johan Liebert, leading to repercussions for his career and reputation. Despite facing adversity, including the deaths of the hospital director and two other doctors, Dr. Tenma eventually becomes hospital director himself. However, nine years later, his past catches up with him when he encounters Johan again, now a criminal. Dr. Tenma must confront the consequences of his actions and embark on a quest for redemption.

Monster is a chilling tale that delves into uncomfortable subjects like human conditioning and the psychology of sociopaths, set in a world frighteningly similar to reality. Dr. Kenzou Tenma, the protagonist, grapples with moral dilemmas as he navigates a complex web of intrigue and deceit.

The writing is exceptional, combining moments of shock, awe, and depravity with anticipation and proper denouement. The slow burn pace captivates viewers, and the intelligent dialogue adds depth to the characters and plot.

Teaser visual

The art augments the tone and character development, with realistic character designs and stunning background art. The variety in body types and nationalities adds to the authenticity of the world.

The voice acting is outstanding, with performances that express the complexity of emotions and experiences. The sound effects and soundtrack enhance the realism and atmosphere of the series.

Trailer

The depth of the characters, both main and supporting, is a standout feature of Monster. The complex relationship between Dr. Tenma and Johan Liebert drives the narrative, while the supporting ensemble adds intensity and gravity to the story.

Monster is a gripping and thought-provoking series that keeps viewers on the edge of their seats. Its dramatic storyline, intriguing atmosphere, and unique exploration of complex issues make it a modern classic of anime.
