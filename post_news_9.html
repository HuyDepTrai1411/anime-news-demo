The Banished Former Hero Lives as He Pleases Anime Reveals New Visual, April 1 Debut

Studio DEEN, Marvy Jack produce series starring Shōta Aoi

Trương Xuân Huy

The staff for the television anime adaptation of Shin Kouduki's The Banished Former Hero Lives as He Pleases (Dekisokonai to Yobareta Moto Eiyū wa, Jikka Kara Tsuihōsareta node Suki Katte ni Ikiru Koto ni Shita) heroic fantasy light novel series unveiled a new visual on Friday.

The anime is set to debut on TV Tokyo on April 1 at 26:00 (effectively April 2 at 2:00 a.m.), with subsequent airings on AT-X on April 2 and BS TV Tokyo on April 3. Additionally, the series will stream on U-NEXT and Anime Hōdai in Japan and other platforms.

The main cast includes:

  • Shōta Aoi as Allen
  • Minami Kurisaka as Riese
  • Akari Kitō as Anriette
  • Sora Amamiya as Noel
  • Aimi as Mylene
  • Megumi Han as Beatrice
  • Mariya Ise as Akira
  • Takehito Koyasu as Craig
  • Ryota Ohsaka as Brett
  • Kōki Koyasu as Curtis

Teaser visual

The anime is directed by Kazuomi Koga (TenPuru) at Studio DEEN and Marvy Jack. Rintarou Ikeda (Insomniacs After School, The Seven Deadly Sins: Imperial Wrath of The Gods) is overseeing the series scripts, with Yoshiki Ōkusa (Hakyū Hōshin Engi, Farewell, My Dear Cramer) as the scriptwriter. Saori Hosoda (Phantom of the Idol) is responsible for character design, and Kei Haneoka (Kono Oto Tomare!: Sounds of Life, Owarimonogatari, Kaguya-sama: Love is War) is composing the music. Shōta Aoi performs the opening theme song "Evolve."

J-Novel Club has licensed both the novels and their manga adaptation. The story follows Allen, a former hero who, despite being banished for his lack of a god-given Gift, finds himself drawn into a new heroic saga when he encounters someone in need.

Trailer

Kouduki began serializing the light novel series on the Shōsetsuka ni Narō (Let's Become Novelists) website in January 2018, with TO Books starting to publish the novels in October 2018. Karasumaru launched an ongoing manga adaptation on the Nico Nico Seiga website in 2019.
