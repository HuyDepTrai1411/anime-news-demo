Roleplaying in Re:Zero, the Dark Souls of Isekai

His roleplay is gradually becoming a reality

Trương Xuân Huy

Watching Re:Zero Starting Life in Another World reminded me of playing Dark Souls, both known for their dark and challenging nature. Both Subaru and Dark Souls players face repeated deaths but grow stronger over time.

However, I'm more interested in discussing roleplay, a form of play not exclusive to action-adventure games. In Dark Souls, I roleplay by protecting NPCs, making choices to ensure their safety. Similarly, Subaru in Re:Zero roleplays to protect his loved ones.

In Dark Souls, I explore NPC questlines, making choices to ensure positive outcomes. I even spoil myself on game details to avoid making decisions that harm NPCs. Similarly, Subaru in Re:Zero endures repeated trauma to protect those he cares for.

Roleplaying allows me to be a better person in games like Dark Souls, where I strive to show kindness in a bleak world. Similarly, Subaru roleplays as a hero in Re:Zero, despite his doubts about himself.

Subaru doubts himself but strives to be a hero to protect Emilia and others. Similarly, I roleplay to save NPCs and show kindness in Dark Souls, despite the game's bleak setting.

Teaser visual

Subaru and Rem both struggle with self-doubt but support each other, believing in the goodness within themselves. Similarly, I find affirmation in the kindness of others and strive to be better.

Roleplaying allows us to cope with our doubts and strive to be better people, both in games like Dark Souls and in real life.

Trailer

With the support of others, Subaru and I continue to strive to be heroes in our own ways, believing in the goodness within ourselves and others.
