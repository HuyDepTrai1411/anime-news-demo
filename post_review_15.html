The First Slam Dunk

THE FIRST SLAM DUNK

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #15

Type: Movie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Dec 3, 2022

Episodes: 1

Premiered: Winter 2022

Synopsis: Shohoku's point guard, Ryouta Miyagi, is known for his lightning speed and strategic gameplay on the basketball court. Hailing from Okinawa, Ryouta followed in the footsteps of his older brother, who was a local basketball star.

In his second year of high school, Ryouta joins the Shohoku High School basketball team alongside Sakuragi, Rukawa, Akagi, and Mitsui. Together, they prepare to compete in the Inter-High School National Championship, with their sights set on challenging the reigning champions, Sannoh Kogyo High School.

This live-action adaptation of Slam Dunk falls short in capturing the essence of the original anime and manga. While the movie attempts to present the characters and storyline, it lacks depth and fails to evoke the same atmosphere.

The music, although adequate, often leaves moments feeling awkwardly silent, especially during critical scenes such as championship games where the absence of cheering undermines the fullness of the crowd. The pacing, while initially fast-paced and realistic, is disrupted by frequent flashbacks and occasional slow-motion sequences, diminishing the tension and excitement of the matches.

Teaser visual

Furthermore, the portrayal of characters, including the protagonist team Shohoku and their rivals, feels hollow and lacks the depth seen in the original series. The interactions between characters, known for their humor and wholesomeness in the anime and manga, seem forced and devoid of charm.

While the focus on Miyagi Ryouta's background is appreciated, the movie could have benefited from a more concentrated narrative or improved execution. The absence of the lively atmosphere and endearing character dynamics present in the source material leaves much to be desired.

Trailer

In conclusion, while the bold approach of the live-action adaptation is commendable, it ultimately falls short in several key areas. A shorter OVA format or a more focused storyline could have potentially addressed these shortcomings and delivered a more satisfying viewing experience.
