Orange

オレンジ

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #8

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Jul 4, 2016 to Sep 26, 2016

Episodes: 13

Premiered: Summer 2016

Synopsis: Naho Takamiya's first day of her sophomore year of high school is off to an uneasy start. After waking up late, she receives a strange letter addressed to her. However, the letter is from herself—10 years in the future! At first, Naho is skeptical of the note; yet, after witnessing several events described to take place, she realizes the letter really is from her 26-year-old self.

The note details that Naho's future life is filled with regrets, and she hopes that her younger self can correct the mistakes that were made in the past. The letter also warns her to keep a close eye on the new transfer student, Kakeru Naruse. Naho must be especially careful in making decisions involving him, as Kakeru is not around in the future. With the letter as her guide, Naho now has the power to protect Kakeru before she comes to regret it once more.

School life provides a captivating backdrop for romance, and "orange" takes this familiar setting and adds a unique twist, making it stand out from typical shoujo anime.

The storyline follows Naho, a 16-year-old who receives letters from her future self, urging her to prevent her "biggest regret" - the death of her classmate, Kakeru. Despite initial skepticism, Naho begins to heed the letters' warnings, altering events to save Kakeru from his fate. As the story unfolds, it delves into themes of friendship, love, and the consequences of altering destiny.

Teaser visual

However, the time travel mechanism remains a mystery, leaving some unanswered questions and detracting from the overall execution of the plot. While the focus is primarily on the main characters, secondary characters play supportive roles but lack significant development.

The main trio - Kakeru, Naho, and Suwa - drive the narrative, each grappling with their own emotions and relationships. Kakeru's portrayal of depression adds depth to his character, while Naho's determination to save him reflects her selflessness. Suwa's unrequited love for Naho adds complexity to the love triangle dynamic, although secondary characters receive less attention.

The animation quality is mediocre, with sluggish character movements detracting from the overall visual experience. While the background designs are decent, the character designs could be improved to enhance immersion.

Trailer

The opening and ending themes complement the show's emotional tone, setting the mood for each episode. Despite its flaws, the soundtrack effectively enhances the viewer's emotional engagement.

Despite its shortcomings, "orange" delivers an emotionally engaging experience, with each episode offering a rollercoaster of feelings. While the manga may provide a more comprehensive depiction of the story, the anime adaptation is still worth watching.

Despite its flaws, "orange" is a compelling watch, exploring themes of love, friendship, and destiny in a school setting. While it may not be the best adaptation, its emotional depth and relatable characters make it a worthwhile addition to the romance genre.
