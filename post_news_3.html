The Idolm@ster Shiny Colors TV Anime Premieres April 5

Anime screened in theaters as anime films between October to January

Trương Xuân Huy

The announcement for The IDOLM@STER Shiny Colors anime came on Saturday, revealing its television premiere scheduled for April 5 along with the release of a new trailer.

Before its television debut, the anime screened in theaters as three separate films. The first film premiered in Japan from October 27 to November 16, earning approximately 40,284,350 yen (about US$268,100) during its opening weekend. The second film followed, running from November 24 to December 14, while the third film aired from January 5 to January 25. These films were showcased in 75 theaters across 47 prefectures in Japan.

Directed by Mankyū (known for works like Idolm@ster Cinderella Girls Theater and The Ice Guy and His Cool Female Colleague), the anime is produced by Polygon Pictures. Yoichi Kato, supervising and writing series scripts, brings his experience from The IDOLM@STER Million Live! and Aikatsu! to the project. The anime's opening theme song, "Tsubasa Gravity," has also been announced.

Teaser visual

The IDOLM@STER Shiny Colors originated as a browser game launched by Bandai Namco Entertainment in April 2018. In this free-to-play game, players train idols and engage in online competitions against other producers/players in live concert performances. The project expanded to include concert events, merchandise, CDs, and radio programs.

Trailer

Last April, Bandai Namco Entertainment unveiled The IDOLM@STER Shiny Colors: Song for Prism, a smartphone game that further expands the franchise's reach.
