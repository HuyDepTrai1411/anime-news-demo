Usagi Drop

Bunny Drop

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #6

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Jul 8, 2011 to Sep 16, 2011

Episodes: 11

Premiered: Fall 2011

Synopsis: Daikichi Kawachi is a 30-year-old bachelor working a respectable job but otherwise wandering aimlessly through life. When his grandfather suddenly passes away, he returns to the family home to pay his respects. Upon arriving at the house, he meets a mysterious young girl named Rin who, to Daikichi's astonishment, is his grandfather's illegitimate daughter!

The shy and unapproachable girl is deemed an embarrassment to the family, and finds herself ostracized by her father's relatives, all of them refusing to take care of her in the wake of his death. Daikichi, angered by their coldness toward Rin, announces that he will take her in—despite the fact that he is a young, single man with no prior childcare experience.

Raising a child is a demanding journey, filled with sacrifices and worries, yet anime rarely explores this aspect of life. "Usagi Drop" defies this trend, adapting a poignant story of parenthood from the manga by Unita Yumi.

Kawachi Daikichi, a 30-year-old salesman, takes in his grandfather's illegitimate daughter, Kaga Rin, after family disputes. The anime stays true to the source material, portraying the complexities of parenting and the evolving relationship between Daikichi and Rin.

Teaser visual

The artwork, characterized by stylized characters and expressive emotions, complements the narrative's maturity. While the pacing may feel slow at times, the lack of melodrama allows for a deeper exploration of themes.

The voice acting enhances the natural script, with standout performances from the cast. Daikichi's character development, alongside Rin's gradual growth, forms the heart of the story.

Trailer

The supporting characters add depth to the narrative, emphasizing bonds and interactions free from unnecessary angst. "Usagi Drop" offers a simple yet charming portrayal of parenthood, resonating with mature audiences seeking realism over escapism.

Despite its departure from conventional anime tropes, its emphasis on authenticity and emotional depth sets it apart, making it a rare gem in the genre.
