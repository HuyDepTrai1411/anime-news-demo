Natsume Yuujinchou

Natsume's Book of Friends

Trương Xuân Huy

Ranked: #11

Type: TV Serie

Status: Finished Airing

Aired: Jul 8, 2008 to Sep 30, 2008

Episodes: 13

Premiered: Summer 2008

Synopsis: Takashi Natsume, due to his ability to see youkai, has always felt isolated and misunderstood. When he accidentally breaks a barrier, he frees Madara, a powerful spirit who notices Natsume's resemblance to his late grandmother, Reiko Natsume. Reiko was known for creating the Book of Friends, which contains the names of youkai she defeated. Natsume decides to keep the book to honor his grandmother's memory and protect it from youkai who seek its power. He makes a deal with Madara to hand over the book eventually, in exchange for Madara acting as his protector. As Natsume aims to free the youkai sealed in the book, his relationships with both youkai and humans gradually improve.

I eagerly anticipated Natsume Yuujinchou after being captivated by a promotional image featuring Natsume and a mysterious cat by his side. Watching it as it aired and waiting for subtitles only deepened my appreciation for this series, which has become a cherished favorite.

Natsume encounters various youkai in each episode, each with their own story and request. The series delves into the bonds between humans and youkai, offering poignant insights derived from Natsume's experiences. Each episode leaves a bittersweet but warm impression, inviting reflection and rewatching.

Teaser visual

The characters are rich and unique, particularly Natsume, whose deep connection with youkai evolves throughout the series. Madara, initially focused on the Book of Friends, develops a genuine concern for Natsume. Youkai, though appearing briefly, are memorable with well-crafted personalities and struggles.

The artwork's simplicity enhances the series, with warm colors and memorable designs creating a stunning atmosphere. Scenes of Natsume returning youkai's names are particularly captivating.

Trailer

The opening and ending songs, along with the soundtrack, complement the atmosphere perfectly, enhancing the viewing experience and becoming integral to the series' charm.

Natsume Yuujinchou is a heartwarming and relaxing anime that is sure to enchant viewers seeking a compelling and thoughtful series.
